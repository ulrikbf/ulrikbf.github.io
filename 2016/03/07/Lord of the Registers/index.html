1 Registers

What can i say, I've never really been quite comfortable with using registries in either Emacs or Vim. I think it has something to do with the way they were introduced to me, but it's been such a long time i really cant remember. Either way, i found myself in a situation where registers really could be beneficial , so i thought to myself: Just learn it, god damnit!. So i did.

1.1 Registers

Registers are like small buffers for holding different pieces of text. It's like have several clipboard you can paste from, and it's actually really neat. You define a new entry in the registers by typing C-x r x and choose a name for the register. Later, you can cycle through the registers with helm (spacemacs) and just type ~SPC r ~ and choose your which entry to insert. You can also copy your cursor point into a registry and jump back or cycle between points later.

1.2 Book marks

Book marks are somewhat like registers in that they record positions you can jump to. Unlike registers, they have long names, and they persist automatically from one Emacs session to the next. The prototypical use of book marks is to record where you were reading in various files. Bookmarks are stored in the file /.emacs.d/bookmarks. You can insert a book mark with C-x r m and move between them with M-m r b.
